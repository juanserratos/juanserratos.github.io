
### Millenials FIRE Movement 

The title to this is meant to be a clickbaity in nature, but done very poorly. But there is pervasive thought I have about financial markets which we all have to believe in to live without working until the moment we're on our death beds. That is, we need to, in essence, believe in the S&P 500, or a bit broader, the United States' financial markets. 

It has become more in vogue to consider retirement at earlier and earlier ages — largely as a result of alienation (in Marx's sense). Take for example the *FIRE movement* (FIRE here stands for 'Financial Independence, Reture Early') [See the [Fire Reddit](https://www.reddit.com/r/Fire/)]. This movement is based on living (typically) frugally earlier in life in order to have high saving rates and aggresively invest these savings with the goal of having enough assets to cover living expenses and, well, you know, retire. This FIRE movement is largely attributed to millenials in the 2010s. The peak millenial who, I think, had a large influence on Gen Z high schoolers was Frank Niu (on Tiktok). He's a self-proclaimed FIRE advocate and was one of the main people adovcating the relaxed SWE lifestyle — his advice on tiktok has a high contribution factor to the saturation of computer science degrees, I believe. 

![Frank Niu](blog/images/frankniu.png)


There's even funny terms in the FIRE movement like *Geo-Arb* which basically means to earn money in a strong currency such as the dollar, sterling, etc, and live in a place with low cost of living — the digital nomad lifestyle. Central to the FIRE movement's thesis is the high savings rates at an EARLY age. The idea here is of course to have compounding returns at a younge age because linear savings will be insufficient. For example, consider a \$80,000 salary after tax (starting at age 24) and suppose that you would be able to save \$25,000 a year until the age of 37. There are too many things to consider around this age range (e.g. kids) so we'll just stick to these 13 years. Then we'll have saved 13 * \$ 25,000 = \$325,000 which is certainly not enough to retire from the age of 37 onwards. Even if you were about to save around 50% percent of your income during this time period (practically not feasible right now) you'll have saved \$520,000 which still not enough until the end of your life. For the FIRE movement, linearly saving is simply not enough to get to the goal of retiring early
