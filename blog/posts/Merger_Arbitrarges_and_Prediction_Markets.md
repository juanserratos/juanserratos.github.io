


### Mergers

How do David Ellison, Donald Trump, Kalshi, and the rights to *the Sopranos* all come together? Larry Ellison. A story which ties these all together is Larry Ellison's, CEO and co-founder of Oracle, modern push for amassing a huge portfolio of the US media landscape — forming a firm foundation for the consideration of a new Murdoch. I don't know if it was David Ellison, Larry Ellison's son, who convinced Larry that he should spend tens of billions of dollars amassing the US media industry or if it was the other way aroun of "affluent" (Larry was a billionaire at the time of his birth and currently has a net worth of  $ \$245.3$ billion) Californians: being into the film industry and going to USC (also my undergraduate institution!). Later on he dropped out of USC and made a box-office bomb, *Flyboys*, financed by Larry, and went on to do a few other small-time acting roles before switching to the business side of Hollywood. This move into business came in the form of founding *Skydance Media*, which I have to say is a perfect corporate name.  

Skydance Media operated as a media production and financing company which covered some giants like the Mission: Impossible and Top Gun franchises. However, Skydance was still a minnow in comparison to a lot of the giants of the industry: Netflix with a $ \$300$ billion market cap, Disney with $ \$200$ billion, Comcast with $ \$100$ billion, Warner Bros with (now) $ \$20$ billion, and so on. To make itself competitive, Skydance swallowed Paramount (...merged) in 2025 by acquiring the controlling stake of National Amusements' for $ \$2.4$ billion in cash which then gave them decision-making control without buying all economic rights directly. Note: "Skydance Investor Group, comprised of the Ellison Family and RedBird Capital Partners, to invest $ \$2.4$ billion to acquire National Amusements for cash and $ \$4.5$ billion for the stock/cash merger consideration to be paid for publicly traded Class A shares and Class B shares, as well as $ \$1.5$ billion of primary capital to be added to Paramount’s balance sheet."  The mention of the "Ellison family" here is just Larry Ellison's backing. Also: "The transaction combines the Skydance Investor Group’s (“Skydance IG”) financial resources, deep operating experience, and expertise in cutting-edge technology with Paramount’s iconic IP, deep film and television library, proven hit-making capabilities, and linear and streaming platforms that reach millions of viewers." This is all to say that the Ellison family (beyond David), i.e. Larry, will be deeply integrated within Paramount Skydance which holds some politically sensitive jewels like CBS (which has been completely revamped and under a campaign of appeasement to Trump).

Finally Paramount  *merged* its business with Skydance through an all-stock transaction and Skydance equity holders received shares in the new company (*Paramount Skydance*), which effectively valued Skydance’s contribution at $ \$4.75$ billion of equity. At the head of this new company is David Ellison. This *merger* also had to clear regulatory approval, likely with some help from the Trump admin not feeling the need to press on long-time Republican and Trump mega donor Larry Ellison's new ventures. Largely today Paramount looks the same as it was: struggling in the subscription-based media landscape, competing against giants like HBO Max and Netflix.
